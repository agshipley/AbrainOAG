# Garage startup ideas for 10k monthly revenue

## Summary

**Conversation Overview**

They explicitly ruled out consulting, fractional work, legal services sales, and anything requiring persuading individuals to hire them directly.

Claude made repeated attempts that failed to satisfy the person's constraints—cycling through service businesses (legal ops consulting, fractional Chief of Staff, AI agent builds), software marketplaces (Shopify apps, Atlassian plugins), and faceless marketplace models (print-on-demand, digital downloads)—each time anchoring either on the person's existing technical projects or defaulting to software as the assumed medium. The person consistently and directly corrected these anchoring errors, noting that Claude was obsessing over software when a broader world of business exists, and that suggestions kept violating the core constraint of no direct selling or client book building. Claude also erroneously cited a platform called "Algora" as a concrete recommendation without verifying it existed, which the person flagged. By the end of the conversation, after the person relaxed timing assumptions to two months for initial revenue with no specific growth target thereafter and confirmed a budget of up to $5k, Claude landed on a digital product storefront concept—professional financial models, document toolkits, and structured reference products for early-stage operators, sold via Gumroad with paid search traffic to compress the ramp timeline.

The person confirmed they could produce at expert quality: spreadsheet and financial models (cap tables, SaaS metrics, deal models), document toolkits (templates, checklists, operational playbooks), and structured data or research products. They have a stated preference for ideas that generate faceless, recurring transactions through existing platform demand rather than through any personal sales motion. Key correction patterns the person established: Claude should not anchor on assets the person has already built unless explicitly invited to, should not default to software as the assumed medium, should not restate constraints back as if that constitutes analysis, and should commit to a concrete direction rather than presenting optionality and asking the person to choose.
