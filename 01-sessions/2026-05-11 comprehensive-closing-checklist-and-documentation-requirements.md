# Comprehensive closing checklist and documentation requirements

## Summary

**Conversation Overview**

Andrew is an attorney at A.G. Shipley Law serving as seller's counsel on a stock purchase transaction involving RevGenius, Inc. (seller) and RevGen Holdings, Inc. (buyer, affiliated with SalesAI). The deal involves nine individual selling stockholders, a total purchase price of $750,000 structured as a closing payment, deferred consideration via promissory note, and an earnout. Andrew has been working this deal for approximately eight months and has an established working discipline requiring Claude to read source documents directly rather than rely on memory or prior assertions.

Andrew asked Claude to build a closing checklist from the SPA redline, LOI, and ancillary documents, first as an internal tracking tool and then as a simplified external version for circulation to buyer's counsel. The conversation involved significant iteration and critical feedback. Claude's first version was rejected as over-built and inaccurate: it included irrelevant line items (antitrust analysis, individual tax filing obligations, items that merely restated representations), contained no accurate status information despite substantial work product existing in the project, included an unhelpful deal summary tab, and had a disclosure schedule tracker that was wrong throughout. A rebuilt version was closer but still contained errors Andrew caught by spot-checking individual rows — one row listed only one signatory on a document requiring two, and another included nine individual FIRPTA certificates when the correct deliverable for a non-USRPHC stock sale is a single company-level certificate under §1.1445-2(c)(3), not individual seller affidavits under §1.1445-2(b). Andrew noted this error also means SPA §2.02(e) is mis-drafted and needs correction in the next redline round. Andrew expressed that the pattern of errors requiring him to audit Claude's work is a recurring trust issue, not isolated to this session.

The final external checklist delivered is a single-tab, 26-item document organized into four sections (Transaction Agreement and Exhibits, Seller Closing Deliverables, Buyer Closing Deliverables, Corporate Approvals and Pre-Closing Items) with responsibility and status columns. Key facts Andrew confirmed during the session: Senzu partnership termination was resolved months ago and should not appear anywhere; the AngelList SAFE involves a single instrument with repayment of approximately $100,000 at closing rather than a complex resolution; finalized disclosure schedules are a closing condition but sub-schedules only warrant individual checklist lines if they generate a separate ancillary document. Andrew's core direction throughout was that a closing checklist tracks documents to be signed, delivered, or wired — not deal analysis, not representations repackaged as deliverables, not administrative overhead. Andrew also flagged that the external version should not go out until he confirms whether the SPA ball is in buyer's or seller's court, since that affects how the responsibility column reads on the most visible line item. Key colleagues referenced include Anthony Riley (Buyer CFO, primary negotiating contact) and Thomas Lyons (Buyer General Counsel).
