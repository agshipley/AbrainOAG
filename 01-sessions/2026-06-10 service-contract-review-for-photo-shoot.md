# Service contract review for photo shoot

## Summary

**Conversation Overview**

The person is a co-founder of Daughters NY (DNY), a marketing/creative agency. They asked Claude to review and redline a service contract between DNY and Oishii Farm Corporation, covering a photo shoot. The person had four specific asks: (1) narrow the indemnification clause from a blanket trigger to a fault-based one limited to DNY's own negligence, willful misconduct, or breach; (2) make indemnification mutual on the same terms; (3) add a liability cap tied to total contract value; and (4) establish standard portfolio/promotional use rights for DNY. They also invited Claude's broader observations on the contract.

Claude produced a redlined Word document with tracked changes and margin comments addressing all four asks, plus additional edits: conforming the IP indemnity carve-outs in Section 13.3 to match the existing warranty carve-outs, and flagging (as comments, not redlines) the cancellation/kill-fee economics and the license duration ambiguity in Section 7. The person correctly identified that publicity rights had been edited in Schedule A rather than the main contract body, and pushed back on this approach — twice. Claude initially over-explained why the edit was placed there before conceding the point. The person's clear instruction: material legal terms like publicity rights do not belong in a schedule or SOW; they go in the main contract body.

The final deliverable on publicity rights was standalone clause language (titled "Portfolio and Promotional Use") drafted for insertion after Section 8 (Ownership) in the main body, with instructions to delete the existing self-promotion sentence from Schedule A or replace it with a cross-reference. The person's working style is direct and experienced with contract drafting — they expect Claude to apply standard legal drafting conventions without needing to be told, and they do not want over-explanation when they give a correction.
