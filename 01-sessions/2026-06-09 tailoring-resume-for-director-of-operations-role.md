# Tailoring resume for director of operations role

## Summary

**Conversation Overview**

Andrew Shipley, a senior operator and Chief of Staff with backgrounds in corporate law and experimental psychology, worked with Claude across multiple rounds to develop a professional resume targeting Director of Operations and Chief of Staff roles at technically complex early-stage companies. The conversation began with a recruiter inquiry from Usama Naveed at an unnamed company, which Claude flagged as a potentially low-signal opportunity given the recruiter's evasiveness about the company name and the boilerplate job description. The initial work involved tailoring Andrew's existing resume to the job description, but Andrew pushed back across several rounds, correctly identifying that the output was generic and failed to reflect his distinctive background or voice. Claude acknowledged that optimizing for ATS keyword-matching against a boilerplate JD structurally produces undifferentiated output, and the conversation shifted to treating the exercise as genuine resume development.

The resume went through four major iterations. The flagship version that emerged leads with a thesis paragraph rather than a competency list, includes a dedicated "Selected AI Systems" section positioned prominently (Andrew's explicit preference for founder and applied-AI audiences), and frames his credentials as the engine behind his judgment rather than a trophy shelf. Andrew has four production AI systems: charlie (a multi-agent intelligence and decision-support system with source-traceable reasoning, discrepancy detection, and confidence scoring, deployed for an entertainment-strategy executive); first-agent (a deployed lead-generation and municipal-data system built for Tre Borden & Co, a public-art commissioning practice, validated by a 275-test suite); mrkt (an empirical pipeline modeling M&A deal-term negotiability across 152 agreements); and Orpheus (an open-source job-search engine on a Model Context Protocol architecture). Claude twice failed to include charlie in portfolio summaries before Andrew corrected it; the pattern was noted and should not recur.

Key professional details: Andrew co-founded AGS Law PLLC, a four-lawyer corporate boutique serving as outside GC to 100+ startups, and led more than $250M in strategic transactions. At Trace Machina (AI-safety infrastructure, Sequoia/Samsung Next/Wellington-backed), he served as sole operations, legal, and finance leader, growing ARR 10× to over $1M while cutting burn over 30% and outside legal spend sevenfold. At EeroQ (quantum-computing), he was promoted from outside Special Counsel through AGS Law to executive Chief of Staff, closing foundational IP-licensing deals with Princeton and Michigan State and leading over $10M in financing including an $8M B Capital-led Series Seed. He currently serves as fractional Chief of Staff at Audience Haus, a strategy and GTM agency. Education includes a JD from Yale Law School (2015), a Rhodes Scholarship and DPhil in Experimental Psychology from Oxford (year unconfirmed — a live hold that must be resolved before the resume is sent), a Fulbright at Victoria University of Wellington, and a B.A. from University of Oregon (Phi Beta Kappa). He has three peer-reviewed publications including a 2020 PLOS ONE paper co-authored with John Jost at NYU.

Several items remain open and gated on Andrew's input: confirming the Oxford DPhil was conferred and providing the year (flagged as the highest-risk unresolved item); providing a concrete metric for Audience Haus; confirming whether the mrkt validation labels were attorney-coded specifically; confirming the AGS firm-operations bullet accurately reflects his responsibilities; verifying Audience Haus is still current; and optionally providing the Fulbright year.
