# Deep Dive: IO Interactive, *007 First Light*, and Delphi

## IO Interactive

IO Interactive (IOI) is a Danish studio headquartered in Copenhagen, with additional studios in Malmö, Barcelona, Istanbul, and Brighton. It built its reputation over roughly two decades on the stealth/espionage genre, most notably the *Hitman* franchise, where players control the bald assassin Agent 47 navigating intricate, sandbox-style "social stealth" levels. The most recent iteration, *Hitman: World of Assassination* (and *Hitman 3* before it), is widely regarded as the genre's high-water mark for systemic, replayable mission design.

A key part of IOI's identity is independence. After splitting from Square Enix in 2017, it became a self-owned developer *and* publisher, and it owns its proprietary engine, **Glacier**, which powers both *Hitman* and *007 First Light*. For the Bond game, IOI says Glacier was "supercharged" in partnership with NVIDIA, and the game launched with path tracing and DLSS 4.5 support.

## *007 First Light*

The game launched globally on **May 27, 2026** for PlayStation 5, Xbox Series X|S, and PC (Steam and Epic), with a Nintendo Switch 2 version slated for Q3 2026. It's a single-player, third-person narrative action-adventure that blends stealth, gunplay, driving, and "the art of the bluff."

The hook is that it's a **wholly original Bond origin story** rather than an adaptation of any film or novel. You play a 26-year-old James Bond, a reckless Royal Navy air crewman recruited into a newly revived Double-0 program. After a training mission ends in tragedy, Bond teams with a reluctant mentor named **John Greenway** to expose a conspiracy and a looming coup. The supporting cast pulls in series mainstays (M, Q, Moneypenny) plus new characters.

Some specifics worth noting:
- **Cast:** Patrick Gibson as Bond, Priyanga Burford as M, Lennie James as Greenway, Alastair McKenzie as Q, Kiera Lester as Moneypenny, Noemie Nakai as Miss Roth.
- **Music:** An original theme by Lana Del Rey and longtime Bond film composer David Arnold; in-game score by The Flight.
- **Reception:** It launched to strong reviews, around an 88 Metacritic / 88–89 OpenCritic. Some outlets called it possibly the best Bond game yet.
- **Direction:** Co-directed by IOI CEO Hakan Abrak and Martin Emborg, written by Michael Vogt, with Christie Golden joining IOI as a principal writer.
- **Commercial tie-ins:** Heavy brand integration (OMEGA watch as the "Q-Watch" gadget, Orlebar Brown, Coca-Cola, Herman Miller), echoing the films' product-placement tradition.

The franchise history matters for context: after Activision's non-exclusive Bond games license was revoked following *007 Legends* (2012), Bond went on an extended video-game hiatus. IOI announced the project (then "Project 007") in **November 2020**, moved it into full production after finishing *Hitman 3*, and fully revealed it in June 2025. Abrak has framed it as "just the beginning of our journey with 007," suggesting the same long-tail, live-content approach IOI used for *Hitman*.

## Delphi (Delphi Interactive)

This is the most under-the-radar piece, and the most interesting. In the game's legal credits you'll see the phrase **"Developed in association with Delphi Interactive LLC"** — but Delphi is *not* a developer in the conventional sense. Its actual role was **licensing and structuring the deal**.

**What Delphi is.** Delphi Interactive is a Beverly Hills / Santa Monica-based company (legally also known as having been incorporated as "Mythic LLC") that describes itself as a firm that "licenses global franchises and partners with top independent video game developers to finance and co-publish AAA video games." It operated in near-total stealth for years and is small — reportedly only around a dozen-plus people on LinkedIn even as recently as late 2025.

**What it actually did for Bond.** In 2020, Delphi was the entity that **acquired/licensed the James Bond video-game rights** directly from Danjaq (Barbara Broccoli's U.S. holding company that controls Bond copyrights/trademarks) and MGM. It then brought IO Interactive in to develop and publish the game "in association with" Delphi. So the chain runs: **Danjaq/MGM (rights owners) → Delphi (licensee/financier/structuring partner) → IO Interactive (developer-publisher).** Notably, even though Amazon acquired MGM in 2022, **Amazon Games is not involved** in the game — Delphi's license predated and sits outside that.

**The business thesis.** Delphi's leadership — CEO Casper Daugaard and president Andy Kleinman — have publicly framed the company as a deliberate alternative to what they call gaming's "publishing industrial complex." Their argument is that legacy publishers (EA, Activision, etc.) have become misaligned both with the owners of major IP and with independent AAA studios. Delphi's model is to sit in the middle: license premium IP, provide financing, and pair it with top independent developers, taking inspiration explicitly from Legendary Entertainment and Skydance in film. They distinguish between IP tied to a specific film/actor/novel and what they call "asynchronous franchises" — game franchises that became their own thing (KOTOR, the Arkham series, *Marvel's Spider-Man*, *Baldur's Gate 3*) — which is the model they want to replicate.

**People and money.** Co-founders include Daugaard and Chad Troutwine (a serial entrepreneur and film producer behind Veritas Prep, Codesmith, and Freakonomics Media). The company later hired former Nordisk Games CEO Mikkel Weider as managing partner in Europe and Julien Merceron as CTO, and says it has staff with EA, Rockstar, and Microsoft Gaming backgrounds. On funding, public SEC filings around 2021–2022 showed raises in the ~$17–19 million range; total funding beyond that is undisclosed.

**Where it's going next.** Bond was Delphi's debut and proof-of-concept. More recently (late 2025), Delphi was named as **publisher/developer of a new FIFA-branded game for Netflix**, timed to the 2026 World Cup — its second major licensed-IP play, and confirmation that the Bond deal wasn't a one-off.
