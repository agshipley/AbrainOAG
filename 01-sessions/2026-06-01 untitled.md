## What the Success Payment actually is (§6.02)

It is **not** a right to 10% of the company at exit. It's the value, at an Exit Event, of a **fixed number of shares** — the number that equaled 10% *as of the February 2021 Effective Date* — treated "as if outstanding" at exit. Three features matter:

1. **Exit-only trigger.** It fires only on (i) an IPO, (ii) acquisition of a *majority* of the equity, or (iii) a sale/exclusive-license of all-or-substantially-all assets. A priced seed where Jumpstart takes a minority does **not** trigger it. One of our flagged worries — "make sure it only triggers on a true exit, not on financings" — is already satisfied by the text.

2. **It dilutes.** Because it's a fixed Feb-2021 share count valued as a fraction of the whole at exit, every share issued afterward shrinks NYU's effective slice. The only anti-dilution carve-outs are narrow: NYU is shielded from (i) the dilutive effect of the *first $500K* of paid-in capital, and (ii) option/equity grants to service providers *above 10%* of the cap table.
