Here's an overview of the Mullin vote and Fetterman's broader Senate voting record since taking office in January 2023.

**The Mullin Vote (March 19, 2026)**

Fetterman cast the deciding vote to advance Markwayne Mullin's DHS nomination, crossing party lines on the Senate Homeland Security Committee. The vote was 8-7, with Republican chairman Rand Paul voting against Mullin over a long-running personal feud, making Fetterman's support the margin that sent the nomination to the full Senate. Fetterman said his vote was rooted in his personal working relationship with Mullin and the need to reopen DHS, which had been in a funding lapse. The vote drew sharp backlash from Democrats, with Rep. Brendan Boyle saying Fetterman "needs to go" and other members openly discussing a potential 2028 primary challenge.

**Overall Voting Pattern**

Fetterman has said he votes with Democrats about 91% of the time, but a legislative tracker found he sided with Trump and Republicans about 26% of the time in 2025 — the highest rate among Senate Democrats, having crossed the aisle over 140 times. The key areas where he's broken with his party include:

**Trump Cabinet Nominees:** Fetterman was the only Democrat to vote to confirm Pam Bondi as Attorney General, and he expressed willingness to support Marco Rubio for State, Pete Hegseth for Defense, and Elise Stefanik for UN Ambassador. He was also one of only a handful of Democrats who voted to confirm Kristi Noem as DHS Secretary in 2025. He also drew criticism from the progressive group Demand Justice for confirming some of Trump's judicial nominees.

**Government Shutdowns:** Fetterman voted with Republicans over a dozen times during the longest-ever government shutdown to fund the government, while most Senate Democrats held out. He was ultimately one of eight Democrats who voted to pass the final resolution reopening the government.

**Iran War Powers:** Earlier in March 2026, when the Senate failed to pass a war powers resolution requiring congressional approval for military action against Iran, Fetterman was the only Democrat to vote against it.

**Israel:** Fetterman has been one of the most outspoken pro-Israel voices among Democrats since October 7, 2023, breaking with President Biden's position on withholding military aid and opposing arms sale restrictions.

**Immigration:** Fetterman has become the most vocal backer of Trump's deportation agenda among Senate Democrats, though he has also pushed back on some specifics like the proposed ICE detention facilities in Pennsylvania.

**Where He's Stayed with Democrats:** Fetterman has maintained support for party positions on LGBTQ rights and abortion access. He has consistently opposed any legal restrictions on abortion, including in the third trimester. He has also supported Democratic positions on climate regulations and has sponsored legislation on consumer protections like credit card fees and food safety.

**Approval Ratings:** His net approval among Pennsylvania Democrats has dropped 108 points since his election, from +68 in 2023 to -40 in 2026. His overall approval is buoyed by Republicans, 73% of whom approve of him, while only 22% of Democrats do. He has also missed about 13.7% of Senate roll call votes, well above the median for current senators.

It's a genuinely unusual record — he's been the most frequent Democratic crossover voter on high-profile issues while still voting with Democrats on the majority of routine legislation. Whether that reflects independent-mindedness or a genuine ideological shift depends on where you sit. His 2028 reelection prospects, if he chooses to run, will likely be shaped by whether Pennsylvania voters view it the same way.
