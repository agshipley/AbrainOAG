Here's the map, grouped by which facet of it you're chasing.

**The doomed-kingpin operas (the bullseye matches)**

- *Carlito's Way* (1993) — De Palma, Pacino as an ex-con trying to go straight in NYC and getting pulled under. The single closest cousin: same doomed grandeur, same operatic fatalism, same city.
- *Scarface* (1983) — the rise-and-fall template the whole subgenre answers to. Excess as tragedy.
- *New Jack City* (1991) — Wesley Snipes (Frank White's *King of NY* lieutenant) graduates to *playing* the kingpin, Nino Brown. A near-literal continuation of the energy with one of the same actors.
- *The Long Good Friday* (1980) — Bob Hoskins as a London boss watching his empire detonate over one weekend. The British grandfather of Frank White: charismatic, contradictory, undone.

**NYC grime and atmosphere**

- *State of Grace* (1990) — same year, Hell's Kitchen Irish mob, Penn/Oldman/Harris. Drenched in the same neon-and-shadow city; criminally underseen.
- *Mean Streets* (1973) / *Taxi Driver* (1976) — the Scorsese bedrock Ferrara is always in dialogue with; the moral-rot-of-the-city source code.
- *We Own the Night* (2007) — James Gray, NYC nightclub-and-drug-world crime, family loyalty vs. the law. Gray's whole catalog (*The Yards*, *Little Odessa*) lives in this register.
- *A Most Violent Year* (2014) — Oscar Isaac as a man trying to stay clean inside a dirty business in 1981 NYC. The slow-burn, ambiguous, atmospheric end of the spectrum.

**More Ferrara & Walken**

- *The Funeral* (1996) — Ferrara *and* Walken again, gangster brothers and Catholic damnation. If you want the director and the star reunited, this is it.
- *Bad Lieutenant* (1992) — Ferrara's spiritual-abyss companion piece (the *Deep Cover* double-bill I flagged earlier too).
- *True Romance* (1993) — not a kingpin film, but Walken's single best scene and pure stylized-cool DNA.

**The stylish-cool wing**

- *Miller's Crossing* (1990) — Coens, gangster loyalty as a chess problem; the most formally elegant of the bunch.
- *The Limey* (1999) / *Sexy Beast* (2000) — Soderbergh and Glazer doing lean, stylish, ex-con crime with serious craft.
- *American Gangster* (2007) — Frank Lucas as businessman-criminal, the Frank White "respectable kingpin" contradiction played straight.
