# Partnership termination agreement for Senzu Partners LLC

## Summary

**Conversation overview**

Andrew is an attorney working on a partnership termination agreement between RevGenius Inc. (client, CEO Jared Robin) and Senzu Partners LLC (operating entity of Mychal Richardson, who had been working with Jared and is now departing). The matter is time-sensitive because the Senzu Partnership Agreement is a Material Contract under a pending SPA with a third-party acquirer (referred to as SalesAi), and the termination must be clean before closing. Andrew worked through the document section by section with Claude, making real-time drafting decisions and asking for redrafts of specific provisions before sending the final version to Jared for distribution to Mychal.

The underlying agreements being terminated are a RevGenius Partnership Agreement and MOU both dated September 1, 2025. Senzu's compensation structure included a $3,500/month Service Fee plus a 20% Revenue Share on net new revenue attributable to Senzu's efforts, with a Section 6(b) tail provision for active initiatives that needed to be expressly waived in the termination agreement. The $5,000 Termination Payment was established as fresh consideration for the release and other undertakings. Key people: Jared Robin (RevGenius CEO, Andrew's client), Mychal Richardson (Senzu principal, counterparty). RevGenius is a Delaware corporation; governing law is Wyoming with New York County venue per the underlying agreements.

The starting draft was an employee separation agreement form with light modifications — structurally wrong for a B2B partnership termination. Andrew directed Claude to redraft Section 4 (continuing obligations and IP assignment) and Section 7 (general release) from scratch, with explicit tactical guidance: on the IP assignment, Andrew instructed Claude not to go "belt and suspenders" because he did not want Mychal to perceive leverage where none needed to be signaled. Claude's final pre-send comments flagged: the April 10 expense deadline (likely to pass before execution), the "as may be required" qualifier in the non-disparagement transaction carve-out (suggested dropping it to give Jared flexibility with Buyer), the removal of the forward-looking covenant from Partner Representations (noted as low risk given the mutual release), and the retroactive Termination Date issue if Mychal delays signing.

### Section 1 — Return of Company Property

🟡 **YELLOW**

The concept is fine but the language is lifted verbatim from an employee form (laptops, cellphones, sales samples). Senzu is an LLC that provided strategic advisory services — the relevant property is more likely digital: admin credentials, platform access, shared drives, CRM access, community management tools, and data. Given Senzu's role as "Head of Strategic Initiatives" with broad material access (Section 2 of the Partnership Agreement), this section should be tailored to cover what Senzu actually had access to.

**Suggested redline concept:** Replace equipment references with: return or revocation of all digital access credentials, admin privileges, API keys, and platform logins; deletion of locally stored Company data and Confidential Information; and confirmation that no copies of Company files, community data, or sponsor/partner records have been retained.

### Section 2 — Accrued Fees and Expenses

🔴 **RED**

This is critically underbuilt. The Partnership Agreement provides two compensation streams: (a) $3,500/month Service Fee and (b) 20% Revenue Share on net new revenue attributable to Senzu's efforts. This section addresses only accrued fees and expenses through the Termination Date but is entirely silent on several key issues:

**Revenue share tail:** Section 6(b) of the Partnership Agreement states that upon termination, Senzu continues to be entitled to the "full amount of the Service Fee and any Revenue Share for active initiatives." The separation agreement needs to either (a) buy out this tail obligation with a lump sum, (b) define a sunset period after which no further Revenue Share accrues, or (c) obtain Senzu's express waiver of continuing Revenue Share rights in exchange for consideration. Without this, Senzu arguably retains indefinite revenue share rights on "active initiatives" — a significant problem for the SalesAi closing.

**Definition of amounts owed:** The draft should specify the exact dollar amount of final Service Fee and any accrued but unpaid Revenue Share, or at minimum establish a reconciliation mechanism with a payment deadline.

**Expense submission deadline:** April 10, 2026 (4 days) is tight but acceptable.

**Suggested approach:** Add a subsection that quantifies total separation consideration, includes an express waiver of Section 6(b) tail rights, and confirms that after payment of the specified amount, Senzu has no further compensation claims of any kind under either agreement.

### Section 7 — General Release

**No consideration specified:** A release requires consideration. The draft doesn't clearly identify what Senzu is getting in exchange for releasing all claims. **Missing OWBPA compliance framework (if applicable):** The NY employment-specific revocation rights (21/45-day review period, 7-day revocation) that often accompany ADEA releases are absent here, but since this isn't an employment relationship, they're not needed.
